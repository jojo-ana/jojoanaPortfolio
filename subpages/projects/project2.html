Goals

I wanted to make an informative website on the most recent Persona game from the perspective of a fan.

Process

Competitive analysis and prepping design work

Competetive analysis

As my topic is on the video game series, I decided to look at the source. A couple of websites that are on a similar topic include the official Atlus website on the games and a fanmade website to keep up with anything and everything Persona (and Atlus in general) related. The Persona 3 Website does well to showcase the content succinctly and creatively. The design choices made on the Persona 3 website are not just aesthetically pleasing but also highly functional. It organizes the layout effectively organizes information, ensuring that visitors can easily navigate through the website. Whether it's learning about the game's story, characters, or mechanics, users can find what they're looking for without unnecessary clutter or confusion. Just a small critique to mention is the utilization of centered text for the character descriptions. Additionally, this example is not reflective of the amount of content I would like to conclude/compile. Nonetheless, I like the aesthetic and use of chunking to organize it.

For the Atlus fansite called Persona Central, it does well to engage with the content. It gives off the information and allows it to connect to other content in a very straightforward. While this is a good example of having multiple connections to content, visually it is very standard/minimal. The fanmade website provides a different yet equally valuable experience for fans. Despite its visually minimalistic approach, it does well with seamless navigation; where a click will take you makes sense.

Design of Website Visuals
