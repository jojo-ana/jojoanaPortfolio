Goals

The problem my app addresses is food insecurity and providing audience options/means to access healthy food options via a means to apply/track to food stamps and food banks. The audience I plan to target are parents that are of low-income and are full-time students. Many of those that utilize these services are households with children. At the same time, those that are unable to utilize these services the least, wherever eligible or not, are also full-time students, hence the target audience being a mix of both

Process

Research

The main need for this is being able to access enough good healthy foods even with low finances. One of the main means of aid is SNAPs (Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program). Despite the usefulness of food stamps, eligible people still tend to be off-put from even trying to engage in the signup process due to how complicated and tedious it is. Even after completing the process, there is still the chance of not receiving that much money. Thus a couple of the functions include a pre-screening feature to help the audience see if they are eligible before engaging in the process, an application tracker that includes: estimated processing time, required actions, communication history, and notifications/alerts (mainly to inform/remind the user about the interviews).

If the individual isnâ€™t eligible for food stamps, as many students do not have, it brings the worry of having access to healthier food options for them and their children. So, the app will offer geolocations of food banks in a 5-10 mile radius, their date/times they are open, and food trackers (specifically for: fresh fruits, vegetables, low-fat fish, and poultry/eggs). If the food at food banks do not suffice as a substantial source for the household, there would also be a function that shows coupons/deals for healthy food option, omitting/filtering out any unhealthy, sugar/calorie high deals unless the user opts into them
